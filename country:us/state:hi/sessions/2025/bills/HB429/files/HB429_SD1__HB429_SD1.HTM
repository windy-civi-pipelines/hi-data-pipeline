NO. 429 THIRTY-THIRD LEGISLATURE, 2025 H.D. 1 STATE OF HAWAII S.D. 1 A BILL FOR AN ACT RELATING TO EARLY LEARNING. BE IT ENACTED BY THE LEGISLATURE OF THE STATE OF HAWAII: SECTION 1.  The legislature finds that the cost of childcare is a significant contributor to the high cost of living in Hawaii.  Many working families with young children or those considering having children cannot afford, or must save significantly to afford, a typical monthly childcare expense. The legislature further finds that increasing access to free public preschool education will help working families in the State prepare their children for kindergarten, which would then dramatically improve the child's academic success and ultimate career opportunities.  However, preschools across the State are facing staffing shortages, especially for teachers and teacher assistants in preschool classrooms. Additional services and equipment are also needed to provide a rich classroom environment for Hawaii's young children to learn. Accordingly, the purpose of this Act is to appropriate funds: (1)  To hire teachers and teacher assistants to staff public preschools; and (2)  For the purchase of equipment and services for public preschools. SECTION 2.  There is appropriated out of the general revenues of the State of Hawaii the sum of $ or so much thereof as may be necessary for fiscal year 2025-2026 and $           or so much thereof as may be necessary for fiscal year 2026-2027 for the executive office on early learning; provided that the funds allocated shall be used as follows: (1)  For fiscal year 2025-2026: (A)  $ for       full-time equivalent ( FTE) pre-school teacher positions; (B)  $ for       full-time equivalent ( FTE) educational assistant III positions; (C)  $ for       full-time equivalent ( FTE) twelve-month state office teacher positions; (D)  $ for substitute teachers; (E)  $ for classroom supplies; (F)  $ for intra-state transportation; (G)  $ for subscriptions; (H)  $ for other miscellaneous current expenses; (I)  $ for furniture and furnishings; and (J)  $ for reference books; and (2)  For fiscal year 2026-2027: (A)  $ for       full-time equivalent ( FTE) pre-school teacher positions; (B)  $ for       full-time equivalent ( FTE) educational assistant III positions; (C)  $ for       full-time equivalent ( FTE) twelve-month state office teacher positions; (D)  $ for substitute teachers; (E)  $ for classroom supplies; (F)  $ for intra-state transportation; (G)  $ for subscriptions; (H)  $ for other miscellaneous current expenses; (I)  $ for furniture and furnishings; and (J)  $ for reference books. The sums appropriated shall be expended by the department of education for the purposes of this Act. SECTION 3.  This Act shall take effect on July 1, 2050.
