RELATING TO CONDOMINIUMS.

 

 

BE IT ENACTED BY THE LEGISLATURE OF THE STATE OF HAWAII:

 

     SECTION 1.  The legislature finds that under existing law, unit owners, tenants, and other persons who use condominium property are subject to the declarations and bylaws adopted by their condominium's association and must strictly comply with the covenants, conditions, and restrictions set forth in the condominium's declaration, bylaws, and house rules.  The legislature further finds that while condominium associations must have adequate remedies to enforce compliance, individuals subject to an enforcement action must also receive due process.

     Therefore, the purpose of this Act is to clarify the process for unit owners and tenants to dispute assessments levied by condominium associations and ensure that there is due process when fines are imposed on individuals subject to condominium association enforcement actions.

     SECTION 2.  Chapter 514B, Hawaii Revised Statutes, is amended by adding a new section to be appropriately designated and to read as follows:

     "§514B-     Fines; imposition; appeals; collection.  (a)  An association may levy reasonable fines against a unit owner or tenant of a unit owner for a violation of the declaration, bylaws, house rules, or regulations adopted by the association.  A unit owner or tenant shall be entitled to appeal the imposition of a fine levied pursuant to this section.  The board shall adopt a resolution that establishes an appeals process that includes notice and an opportunity for the unit owner or tenant to be heard and dispute the fine.  The requirements of this section shall control over fine-related provisions in an association's declaration, bylaws, house rules, or regulations.

     (b)  Prior to the imposition of a fine, the association shall provide notice to the unit owner or tenant, as applicable.  The notice shall include:

     (1)  For each act or omission that constitutes a violation and is subject to the fine:

          (A)  A reference to the applicable provision or provisions of the declaration, bylaws, house rules, or regulations that serves as the basis for the fine; and

          (B)  A general description of the violation; and

     (2)  A copy of the appeals process adopted pursuant to subsection (a).

     (c)  A unit owner or tenant may request a hearing before the board within thirty days of receipt of the notice under subsection (b).

     (d)  A unit owner or tenant aggrieved by the decision of the board may file an appeal in the small claims division of the district court of the judicial circuit in which the condominium is located within thirty days of receipt of notice of the decision from the board regarding the fine.

     (e)  A fine imposed for a violation of the declaration, bylaws, house rules, or regulations adopted by the association shall be enforceable and collectible if:

     (1)  The time to initiate an appeal has expired and no appeal has been initiated;

     (2)  The fine has been upheld by the board following a timely appeal and a small claims court case has not been initiated in accordance with subsection (d); or

     (3)  The fine has not been denied by a small claims court within ninety days of the timely initiation of an appeal pursuant to subsection (d).

     (f)  An association shall not charge a unit owner or tenant any attorneys' fees incurred by the association related to a fine imposed for a violation of the declaration, bylaws, house rules, or regulations adopted by the association unless the fine is enforceable and collectible under subsection (e).

     (g)  Nothing in this section shall be deemed to limit the remedies of the association otherwise available under this chapter."

Report Title:

Condominium Associations; Fines; Assessments; Common Expense Assessments; Appeals

 

Description:

Clarifies the authority of condominium associations to impose fines for violations of the declaration, bylaws, house rules, or regulations adopted by the condominium association.  Establishes a process for the unit owner or tenant of the unit owner to appeal the imposition of a fine.  Clarifies the responsibilities of unit owners to pay common expense assessments.  Clarifies the rights and process for unit owners to appeal assessments other than common expense assessments.  Specifies that the Small Claims Division has exclusive jurisdiction over cases arising from condominium disputes regarding fines.  Effective 7/1/3000.  (HD1)
