NO. 72 THIRTY-THIRD LEGISLATURE, 2025 STATE OF HAWAII HOUSE RESOLUTION requesting the governor to take any actions necessary to reestablish aN INTERISLAND ferry system in the state. WHEREAS, Hawaii is an archipelago comprising one hundred thirty-seven islands, six of which have major population centers; and WHEREAS, residents of, and visitors to, the State rely primarily on aircraft for inter-island transportation; and WHEREAS, between December 2007 and July 2009, residents and visitors were provided an additional option for interisland transportation: the Hawaii Superferry; and WHEREAS, the Hawaii Superferry was a high-speed ferry service that efficiently transported persons and property, including motor vehicles, between the islands of Maui and Oahu; and WHEREAS, reestablishing an interisland ferry system in the State would provide residents and visitors with an efficient, cost-friendly alternative to air and barge transportation between islands; now, therefore, BE IT RESOLVED by the House of Representatives of the Thirty-third Legislature of the State of Hawaii, Regular Session of 2025, that the Governor is requested to take any actions necessary to reestablish an interisland ferry system in the State; and BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED that certified copies of this Resolution be transmitted to the Governor and Director of Transportation.
