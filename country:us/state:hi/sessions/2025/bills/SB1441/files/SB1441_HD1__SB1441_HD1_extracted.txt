SB1441 HD1 THE SENATE S.B. NO. 1441 THIRTY-THIRD LEGISLATURE, 2025 S.D. 2 STATE OF HAWAII H.D. 1 A BILL FOR AN ACT RELATING TO THE TRANSITION OF THE OAHU REGIONAL HEALTH CARE SYSTEM FROM THE HAWAII HEALTH SYSTEMS CORPORATION INTO THE DEPARTMENT OF HEALTH. BE IT ENACTED BY THE LEGISLATURE OF THE STATE OF HAWAII: PART I SECTION 1. The legislature finds that Act 212, Session Laws of Hawaii 2021, required the transfer of the Oahu regional health care system (Oahu region) from the Hawaii health systems corporation to the department of health due to the logistical complexities of the Oahu region facilities and the limited but crucial nature of the services these facilities, Leahi Hospital and Maluhia, currently provide.  The legislature further finds that it is more appropriate for the department of health and Oahu region to form a partnership to assist with the needs of patients from the Hawaii state hospital. Accordingly, the purpose of this Act is to: (1)  Repeal the requirement to transfer the Oahu region from the Hawaii health systems corporation to the department of health; (2)  Repeal certain changes to the Hawaii Revised Statutes that were made by Act 212, Session Laws of Hawaii 2021; (3)  Require the Oahu region and the department of health to enter into a formal memorandum of agreement regarding the care of certain psychiatrically stable patients transferred or discharged from the Hawaii state hospital to an appropriate Oahu region facility, subject to certain conditions; and (4)  Require the Oahu region to submit a report to the legislature. SECTION 4.  Chapter 321, part XLVI, Hawaii Revised Statutes, is repealed. PART IV SECTION 5. (a)  No later than November 30, 2025, the Oahu regional health care system and the department of health shall enter into a formal memorandum of agreement by which the Oahu regional health care system accepts psychiatrically stable patients transferred or discharged from the Hawaii state hospital who meet institutional level of care.  The formal memorandum of agreement shall include the following: (1)  Oahu regional health care system staff shall clinically assess all patients referred for admission by the Hawaii state hospital; and (2)  Hawaii state hospital staff shall support Oahu regional health care system staff with on-call access to psychiatric and related services and on-site psychiatric consultation as medically necessary for patients admitted to Leahi hospital from the Hawaii state hospital on a twenty-four-hour, seven-day-a-week basis. (b)  The Oahu regional health care system shall submit a report to the legislature no later than twenty days prior to the convening of the regular session of 2026. The report shall cover the period of December 1, 2025, through November 30, 2026, and shall include: (1)  The total number of patients referred to the Oahu regional health care system from the Hawaii state hospital; (2)  The number of referred patients who were admitted to Leahi hospital; (3)  For each referred patient who was not admitted: (A)  The date the referral was made; (B)  The date the Oahu regional health care system staff conducted a clinical assessment of the referred patient; (C)  The date the Oahu regional health care system notified the Hawaii state hospital that the patient was not accepted for admission; (D)  Leahi hospital nursing facility patient census on the date the Hawaii state hospital was notified the patient was not accepted for admission; and (E)  A deidentified summary explaining why the patient was not accepted; (4)  The minimum and maximum Leahi hospital nursing patient census for each month; and (5)  Recommendations on how the Oahu regional health care system will be able to admit more patients referred from the Hawaii state hospital. SECTION 6.  Statutory material to be repealed is bracketed and stricken.  New statutory material is underscored. SECTION 7.  This Act shall take effect on July 1, 3000.
